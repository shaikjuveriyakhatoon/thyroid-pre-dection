Foods to Include

For Pregnant Women

  • Iodine-Rich Foods: Include iodized salt, fish (like salmon), and seaweed to support thyroid function.
  • Selenium-Rich Foods: Eggs, sunflower seeds, and Brazil nuts help in thyroid hormone production.
  • Iron-Rich Foods: Leafy greens (like spinach), lentils, and fortified cereals to prevent anemia and support thyroid health.
  • Vitamin D: Include fortified milk, orange juice, and safe sunlight exposure.
  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Include walnuts, chia seeds, and fatty fish for anti-inflammatory benefits.

For Non-Pregnant Women

  • Cruciferous Vegetables (in moderation): Broccoli, kale, and cauliflower are good but should be cooked to reduce goitrogens.
  • Whole Grains: Oats, quinoa, and brown rice for sustained energy levels.
  • Protein-Rich Foods: Chicken, tofu, and legumes for muscle and hormonal balance.
  • Antioxidant-Rich Foods: Berries, nuts, and dark chocolate to reduce oxidative stress.

Foods to Avoid

  • Excessive Soy: Soy milk, tofu, and soy-based products can interfere with thyroid hormone absorption.
  • Processed Foods: High-sugar or high-fat processed snacks can worsen thyroid symptoms.
  • Raw Cruciferous Vegetables: Avoid excessive raw broccoli, cabbage, and kale.
  • Alcohol and Caffeine: Limit intake to avoid disrupting thyroid function.

General Tips for Thyroid Health

  • Stay hydrated with plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Maintain a balanced diet with regular meals.
  • Consult a healthcare provider for personalized nutrition advice.
